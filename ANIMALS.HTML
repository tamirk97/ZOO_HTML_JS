Lion: A large carnivorous mammal, known for its majestic mane and powerful roar. Lions are apex predators, found primarily in sub-Saharan Africa and a small population in India. They live in social groups called prides and are renowned for their strength, agility, and hunting prowess.



Rabbit: A small mammal belonging to the Lagomorpha order, characterized by long ears, a short tail, and strong hind legs adapted for leaping. Rabbits are known for their prolific breeding and are found in diverse habitats across the globe, including forests, grasslands, and urban areas. They primarily feed on vegetation and are preyed upon by various predators.



Flamingo: A tall, wading bird with vibrant pink plumage, long legs, and a distinctively curved beak. Flamingos are known for their striking appearance and often gather in large flocks in shallow waters, where they filter-feed on algae and crustaceans. They inhabit various regions worldwide, including Africa, Asia, Europe, and the Americas.



Snake: A limbless reptile characterized by its elongated body, scaly skin, and forked tongue. Snakes come in a variety of species, ranging from harmless to venomous, and inhabit a wide range of ecosystems worldwide, including forests, deserts, and aquatic environments. They play important roles in ecosystems as both predators and prey.



Horse: A large, herbivorous mammal known for its strength, speed, and domestication by humans for various purposes, including transportation, agriculture, and recreational activities. Horses have a long history of interaction with humans and are valued for their intelligence, loyalty, and versatility. They come in various breeds and are found worldwide.
